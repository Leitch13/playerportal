# Strategy Call Script & Framework

**Business:** PlayIt Love It -- Football Coaching Mentorship
**Founder:** John Leitch, Aberdeen, Scotland
**Call Duration:** 30 minutes
**Format:** Zoom (camera on)

---

## How to Use This Document

Print this out. Have it next to you during every call. Don't read it word for word -- that sounds robotic and people can tell. Use it as a framework. Know the structure, have the key phrases in your head, and let the conversation flow naturally.

**The mindset going in:** You're a coach helping another coach figure out their next step. Not a salesman. If you can help them, say so. If you can't, say that too. The best close is genuine belief that you can solve their problem.

## The Call: 30-Minute Framework

---

### Phase 1: Rapport (3 minutes)

**Goal:** Break the ice. Make them comfortable. Show you're a real person, not a sales machine.

**Opening line options:**

- "Alright {{name}}, good to finally chat properly. How's it going?"
- "Hey, nice to meet you. Thanks for booking this in. How's your day been?"
- "Good to see you mate. Whereabouts are you based again?"

**Light questions to warm up:**

- "Have you been coaching this weekend?"
- "How long have you been following me on Instagram / how did you find me?"
- "Did you get a chance to read through the free guide?"

**Key things to do in this phase:**
- Smile. Camera on. Look at the camera, not yourself.
- Use their name.
- Find something in common (location, coaching background, a shared frustration).
- Keep it brief -- don't spend 10 minutes on small talk. 2-3 minutes max.

**Transition to discovery:**

"Right, let's make sure we use this time well. I want to hear about where you're at and where you want to go, and then I'll give you my honest take on what I think would help. Sound good?"

---

### Phase 2: Discovery (10 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand their situation deeply. LISTEN more than you talk. This is where you earn the right to recommend something later.

**The 6 key questions (ask them all, in roughly this order):**

---

**Question 1: "Tell me about your setup right now. What does your coaching business look like today?"**

Listen for: Number of players, number of sessions per week, how long they've been running, solo or with other coaches, what programmes they offer.

Follow-up probes:
- "How many players are you working with at the moment?"
- "How many sessions a week?"
- "Is this your full-time thing or are you doing it alongside something else?"

---

**Question 2: "Where do you want to be in 12 months?"**

Listen for: A specific number (players, income, sessions), or a vague "I just want to grow." If vague, push for specifics.

Follow-up probes:
- "What does that look like in terms of numbers?"
- "Is the goal to go full-time? Or to grow what you've already got?"
- "What would need to be true for you to feel like this is properly working?"

---

**Question 3: "What's the biggest thing holding you back right now?"**

Listen for: The real blocker. Often it's not what they say first. Let them talk.

Follow-up probes:
- "If you could fix one thing tomorrow, what would it be?"
- "What keeps you up at night about this?"
- "Is it a knowledge thing, a time thing, or a confidence thing?"

---

**Question 4: "What have you tried so far?"**

Listen for: What they've already done that hasn't worked. This tells you their level of sophistication and where the gaps are.

Follow-up probes:
- "Have you tried any marketing -- social media, flyers, anything?"
- "Have you ever paid for advertising?"
- "Have you looked into any courses or coaching before?"

---

**Question 5: "How long have you been coaching?"**

Listen for: Experience level. A coach with 10 years of experience needs different help than someone with 1 year.

---

**Question 6: "What made you book this call today?"**

Listen for: The emotional trigger. Something happened that made them take action. This is the thing you'll reference when you present the solution.

Follow-up probes:
- "What was it that made you think 'I need to do something different'?"
- "Was there a specific moment where you thought 'enough is enough'?"

---

**CRITICAL: Take notes during this phase.** Write down:
- Their current numbers
- Their 12-month goal
- Their main blocker
- What they've already tried
- The emotional trigger

You'll use all of this in the next phase.

**What NOT to do in discovery:**
- Don't interrupt them
- Don't jump to solutions yet ("Oh I can help with that!")
- Don't talk about yourself or your story yet
- Don't minimise their struggles ("That's easy to fix")
- Don't rush through the questions to get to the pitch

---

### Phase 3: Diagnose (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Show them you understand their situation better than they do. Reflect back what they said, identify the gaps, and position yourself as the person who can see what they can't.

**The structure: Reflect, Diagnose, Bridge**

---

**Step 1: Reflect back what they said**

"Right, so let me make sure I've got this. You're at {{number}} players, running {{number}} sessions a week, and you want to get to {{their goal}} in the next 12 months. The main thing that's frustrating you is {{their blocker}}. Have I got that right?"

*Wait for them to confirm. This builds trust -- they feel heard.*

---

**Step 2: Diagnose the gaps**

Pick 2-3 specific gaps based on what they've told you. Don't be vague. Be specific.

**Framework phrases:**

- "Here's what I think is going on..."
- "The reason you're stuck at {{number}} is because..."
- "I've seen this exact pattern with a lot of coaches..."
- "The gap I see is between what you're doing and what would actually move the needle..."

**Common diagnoses (match to what they've told you):**

| If they say... | Your diagnosis is... |
|---|---|
| "I can't get new players" | "You don't have an acquisition system. You're relying on word of mouth and random posts." |
| "Players keep leaving" | "You haven't got a retention strategy. No parent communication rhythm, no progress tracking, families don't feel connected." |
| "I'm working all the time" | "You're doing everything yourself because you don't have systems. You're coaching AND running the business AND doing the admin." |
| "I don't know how to price" | "You're almost certainly undercharging. And your pricing structure probably isn't set up to encourage commitment." |
| "I've tried Facebook ads" | "Paid ads without a proper funnel is just burning money. You need a trial system that converts before you spend on ads." |
| "My area is competitive" | "Competition is actually a good sign -- it means there's demand. The question is what makes you different and how you communicate that." |
| "I want to go full-time" | "You need to know your numbers. What's the break-even? What's the plan to get there? Right now you're guessing." |

**Example diagnosis (put it together):**

"The reason you're stuck at 30 players is two things. First, you don't have a system for getting new enquiries consistently -- you're posting on Facebook and hoping, which isn't a strategy. Second, and this is the bigger one, you're probably losing players you don't need to lose because there's no communication system with parents. If we fixed just those two things, you'd be at 50-60 within a few months. That's not a guess -- that's what I've seen work."

---

**Step 3: Bridge to the solution**

"The good news is, none of this is complicated. You don't need to be a marketing genius. You just need the right system and someone to keep you on track."

---

### Phase 4: Present (7 minutes)

**Goal:** Show them what's possible. Match the solution to their specific situation. Make it tangible.

**The structure: Recommend, Example, Future-Paint**

---

**Step 1: Recommend**

"Here's what I'd suggest..."

Match their situation to the right offering:

| Their situation | Recommend |
|---|---|
| Just starting out, tight budget | The Playbook (group) |
| Has 20-50 players, wants to grow | 1-to-1 mentorship |
| Has 50+ players, wants to scale/systemise | 1-to-1 mentorship |
| Not sure, needs to think | Strategy call value + free guide, follow up later |

**Key phrases:**

- "Based on everything you've told me, I think the best fit for you is..."
- "Here's why I'm recommending this specifically..."
- "For someone at your stage, this is what's going to move the needle fastest..."

---

**Step 2: Share a relevant example**

Use your own story or a mentee's story that mirrors their situation.

"When I was at the same stage as you, I was doing the exact same thing -- running sessions, posting on Facebook, hoping people would show up. What changed everything for me was..."

"I had a coach join recently who was in a really similar position -- {{number}} players, coaching everything himself, no systems. Within {{timeframe}}, he'd {{result}}."

**Make it specific. Vague success stories don't convince anyone.**

---

**Step 3: Paint the future**

Help them see what 3-6 months looks like with the right support.

"Here's what I think is realistic for you. In the next 3 months, if you follow the system, you could..."

**Be specific to their numbers:**

- "Go from 30 to 60-70 players"
- "Get your pricing right and add an extra {{amount}} a month without a single new player"
- "Launch a holiday camp and add a new revenue stream"
- "Hire your first coach so you're not on the pitch every single session"
- "Have a trial system that means enquiries actually convert instead of disappearing"

**Don't overpromise. Underpromise and overdeliver.**

---

### Phase 5: Close (5 minutes)

**Goal:** Present the options, handle any final objections, and get a clear next step.

---

**Step 1: Present the options**

"So there are two ways we can work together."

**Option 1: The Playbook (Group)**
"The first is The Playbook -- it's a group programme where you get the full system, step by step. Weekly modules, all the templates, a weekly group Q&A call with me, and a community of other coaches doing the same thing. The investment is {{price}}."

**Option 2: 1-to-1 Mentorship**
"The second option, and honestly the one I think is right for you based on what you've told me, is the 1-to-1 mentorship. That's you and me, working on YOUR business specifically. Fortnightly calls, direct WhatsApp access to me between calls, a personalised action plan, and proper accountability. The investment is {{price}} for {{duration}}, or {{payment_plan}} per month."

**Then shut up. Let them respond.**

---

**Step 2: Handle final objections**

If they raise an objection, use this framework: **Acknowledge, Reframe, Question**

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I need to think about it" | "Of course. What specifically do you want to think over? Sometimes talking it through helps." |
| "It's a lot of money" | "I get that. Let me ask you this -- what would it be worth to you to have {{their goal}} sorted in the next 6 months? If the mentorship gets you there, is it worth the investment?" |
| "I need to check with my partner" | "Makes sense. Would it help if I put together a quick summary you can show them? What do you think their main concern would be?" |
| "Can I try the group first?" | "Absolutely. The group gives you the system -- you just implement it yourself with group support rather than 1-to-1 attention. It's a great starting point." |
| "I've been burned before" | "Fair enough. What happened? ... Right, here's how this is different: I'm a football coach who built a football coaching business. Not a generic business coach. Everything is specific to what you're actually doing." |

---

**Step 3: Get a clear next step**

**If yes:**
"Brilliant. Here's what happens next. I'll send you {{payment link / onboarding details}}. Once that's sorted, we'll book our first proper call and get started on your action plan. You'll have access to {{resources}} straight away."

**If not yet (but warm):**
"Tell you what -- take a couple of days. I'll send you a message on {{day}} to see where you're at. In the meantime, think about {{specific thing from the call}}. No pressure."

**If no (politely):**
"No worries at all. Honestly, I'd rather you were 100% sure than jump into something you're not ready for. Here's what I'd suggest doing on your own in the meantime..."

Give them 1-2 specific, actionable things they can do for free:
- "Send personal messages to every parent this week"
- "Review your pricing against your competitors"
- "Set up a simple trial process"

"And if things change and you want to revisit it, just drop me a message. Door's always open."

---

**Always end the call with:**

"Cheers for taking the time today {{name}}. Really enjoyed chatting. I think you've got a lot of potential and I'm genuinely excited to see where you take this, whether we end up working together or not."

## Call Preparation Checklist

Before every strategy call, review:

- [ ] Their name and where they're based
- [ ] How they found you (Instagram, referral, ad, etc.)
- [ ] Any previous messages or interactions
- [ ] Their Instagram/Facebook profile (what are they posting, how many followers, do they look established or new?)
- [ ] Any emails they've replied to or lead magnet they've downloaded
- [ ] Your availability for new mentees this month
- [ ] Current pricing and payment plan details

**Tech check:**
- [ ] Zoom link works
- [ ] Camera and mic working
- [ ] Quiet environment, no distractions
- [ ] Notepad and pen ready (or notes app open)
- [ ] This framework printed or visible on a second screen

## Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Show-up rate | 80%+ | If below, improve confirmation/reminder messages |
| Call-to-close rate (same day) | 20-30% | If below, review your diagnosis and close phases |
| Call-to-close rate (within 7 days) | 40-50% | Follow-up sequence matters |
| Average call length | 25-35 min | If calls run long, tighten discovery. If too short, you're not listening enough. |
| Calls per week | 3-5 | More than this and you're spending too much time selling vs delivering |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Talking too much in discovery.** Your job is to listen. Aim for 70% them, 30% you in the first half of the call.

2. **Skipping the diagnosis.** If you go straight from discovery to "here's what I sell," it feels like a sales pitch. The diagnosis is where trust is built.

3. **Being vague in the future-paint.** "You'll grow your business" means nothing. "You'll go from 30 to 60 players and add a holiday camp that brings in an extra two grand" means something.

4. **Not asking for the sale.** Being helpful is great. But if you never present the offer, they'll leave the call without a clear next step. It's not pushy to say "here's how I can help."

5. **Chasing people who aren't a fit.** If they clearly can't afford it, aren't serious, or don't have the basics in place -- be honest. Help them with what you can for free and move on. Your time is better spent with people who are ready.

6. **Not following up.** Half your conversions will happen in the follow-up, not on the call. Don't drop the ball after hanging up.
