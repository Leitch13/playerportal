**Business:** PlayIt Love It -- Football Coaching Mentorship
**Founder:** John Leitch, Aberdeen, Scotland
**Channels:** Instagram DM, Facebook Messenger, WhatsApp

---

## How to Use This Document

These are **starting points**, not scripts to copy and paste word for word. Read the template, adapt it to the person you're speaking to, and keep it natural. The goal is always the same: be helpful first, sell second.

**Golden rules:**
- Reply within 2 hours (ideally under 30 minutes)
- Use their name
- Keep messages short -- these are DMs, not emails
- Voice notes work brilliantly on WhatsApp and Instagram (people connect with your voice)
- Never send a wall of text. Break it into 2-3 short messages if needed
- Always end with a question -- keep the conversation going

## Scenario 1: Cold DM From Someone Who Just Followed

**Context:** A coach follows you on Instagram or connects on Facebook. They haven't messaged you yet. You're making the first move.

**The approach:** Acknowledge, give value, ask a question. Never pitch on the first message.

---

### Variation 1 (Warm and curious)

> Hey {{name}}, cheers for the follow mate. Always good to connect with other coaches.
>
> Quick question -- are you running your own sessions or working for a club?

---

### Variation 2 (Value-first)

> Hey {{name}}, thanks for following! Saw you're into coaching -- class.
>
> I put together a free guide on how I went from 0 to 300+ players if it's useful: {{link}}
>
> No catch. Just stuff I wish someone had told me when I started.

---

### Variation 3 (Direct and personal)

> Hey {{name}}, appreciate the follow. Had a look at your page -- looks like you're doing some good stuff.
>
> Whereabouts are you based?

---

### Variation 4 (Voice note approach)

Send a 15-20 second voice note:

*"Hey {{name}}, just wanted to say cheers for the follow. I'm John, I run PlayIt Love It up in Aberdeen -- football coaching business. Always good to connect with other coaches. Are you running your own thing or working with a club? Drop me a message, would be good to hear what you're up to."*

---

### Variation 5 (If their profile clearly shows they coach)

> Hey {{name}}, saw you're running sessions in {{area}} -- class. How's it going? How many players have you got at the moment?

---

**If they reply, transition to:**

> Nice one. How long have you been at it?
>
> What's the biggest challenge you're dealing with right now?

Then based on their answer, point them toward the free guide, a specific tip, or suggest jumping on a call.

## Scenario 3: "How Much Is the Mentorship?"

**Context:** Someone messages asking about price. This is a buying signal -- they're interested. But giving the price without context leads to sticker shock.

**The approach:** Appreciate the question. Ask qualifying questions first so you can recommend the right option. Then present price with context.

---

### Variation 1 (The standard response)

> Great question, and it depends on what would actually suit you best -- I've got a couple of options.
>
> Can I ask a few quick questions first so I can point you in the right direction?
>
> 1. How many players have you got at the moment?
> 2. What's your main goal -- are you trying to get started, grow your numbers, or build a team?
> 3. Have you been coaching long?

---

### Variation 2 (More casual)

> Aye no worries, I'll give you all the details. But before I do -- tell me a bit about where you're at?
>
> Like, are you just starting out or have you already got sessions running?

---

### Variation 3 (If they seem impatient or direct)

> Totally fair to ask. Short version: I've got a group option and a 1-to-1 option, and the investment depends on which one makes sense for you.
>
> Rather than just throw numbers at you, would you be up for a quick 15-min call? I can explain both options and you can decide if either is the right fit. No obligation at all.
>
> {{booking_link}}

---

### Variation 4 (If you can tell from their profile they're early-stage)

> Hey, cheers for asking. I've got a couple of different options depending on where you're at.
>
> From your page it looks like you're still quite early on -- is that right? How many players have you got at the moment?
>
> Once I know a bit more about your situation I can tell you which option would actually be worth it for you. Don't want to recommend something that's not the right fit.

---

### Variation 5 (Voice note)

*"Hey {{name}}, great question. So I've got two ways we can work together -- a group programme and a 1-to-1 mentorship. They're at different price points and honestly which one's right depends on where you're at. Let me ask you a couple of quick things so I can point you the right way. ## Scenario 4: "I'm Interested But..."

**Context:** They've shown interest but hit you with an objection. This is normal. Don't get defensive. Don't hard sell. Just have a conversation.

---

### Objection: "It's Too Expensive"

**Variation 1:**
> I totally get that. It's not a small investment and I'd never want anyone to stretch themselves.
>
> Can I ask though -- what are you currently earning from coaching per month? And what would you need to be earning for it to be worth the investment?
>
> Most coaches I work with make back the cost within 2-3 months just by fixing their pricing and retention. Not promising, just what I've seen.

**Variation 2:**
> Completely understand. Here's how I'd think about it though -- how much is it costing you right now to NOT have this stuff figured out? Like, undercharging, losing players, spending time on things that aren't growing your numbers...
>
> If we fixed even one of those things, what would that be worth over a year?

**Variation 3:**
> Fair enough mate. I've got a payment plan option if that helps spread it out? And the group programme is a lower entry point if the 1-to-1 feels too much right now.
>
> Either way, no pressure. The free guide I sent covers the basics -- you can absolutely make progress with that on your own.

---

### Objection: "Not the Right Time"

**Variation 1:**
> No worries at all. When do you think would be better? I can check in with you then.
>
> In the meantime, have you downloaded the free guide? Plenty in there to keep you moving forward.

**Variation 2:**
> Totally respect that. Can I be honest with you though? I said the same thing to myself for about 18 months. Kept waiting for the "right time."
>
> The right time was when I stopped waiting and got help. But genuinely, no pressure -- when you're ready, I'll be here.

**Variation 3:**
> That's fair. Just one thing to think about -- every month you wait is another month of trial and error that you could skip.
>
> But I'm not here to pressure you. Grab the free guide if you haven't already, and drop me a message whenever you're ready to have a proper chat about it.

---

### Objection: "I Need to Think About It"

**Variation 1:**
> Of course. Take whatever time you need. Is there anything specific you're weighing up? Sometimes talking it through helps.

**Variation 2:**
> Totally fair. What would help you decide? Is there something specific you'd want to know, or is it more about timing/budget?

**Variation 3:**
> No rush at all. I'll be here whenever. Just so you know, I've got {{number}} spots open for the 1-to-1 this month -- not saying that to rush you, just so you've got the full picture.

---

### Objection: "My Area Is Different"

**Variation 1:**
> I hear this a lot. Whereabouts are you based?
>
> I'm in Aberdeen. Not exactly a football hotspot. But we've got 300+ players every week. The system works because parents everywhere want quality coaching for their kids and there's almost always a gap in the market for someone who does it properly.

**Variation 2:**
> What specifically about your area do you think is different? I've spoken to coaches in all sorts of locations -- cities, small towns, rural areas. The principles are the same, you just adapt the details.
>
> Would it help if we jumped on a quick call and I could give you my honest take on your area?

**Variation 3:**
> I built this in Aberdeen, mate. If it works here, it'll work most places. But I get the concern.
>
> Tell me about your area -- what's the competition like? How many kids are there locally? Let me give you a proper answer rather than a generic one.

## Scenario 6: Testimonial Request

**Context:** A mentee is getting results. You need to capture that as social proof. Don't be awkward about it -- they're usually happy to help.

---

### Initial Testimonial Ask

**Variation 1 (Casual):**
> Hey {{name}}, really buzzing about the progress you've been making. Going from {{old situation}} to {{new situation}} in {{timeframe}} is class.
>
> Would you mind if I shared your story? Nothing fancy -- just a few lines about where you were, what we worked on, and where you are now.
>
> Could be a quick voice note, a few sentences in a message, or even a short video if you're up for it. Whatever's easiest for you.

**Variation 2 (With prompts to make it easy):**
> {{name}}, your results have been incredible mate. I'd love to share your story to help other coaches who are in the same position you were.
>
> Would you be able to answer these 3 questions for me?
> 1. Where were you when we started working together?
> 2. What's changed since then?
> 3. What would you say to a coach who's on the fence about getting support?
>
> Doesn't need to be long. A voice note is totally fine.

**Variation 3 (Direct ask):**
> Hey {{name}}, can I ask a favour? I'm trying to build up some case studies and your progress has been one of the best examples I've got.
>
> Would you be willing to share a few words about your experience? I can use it on my page and website. Only if you're comfortable obviously.

---

### Follow-Up If They Haven't Sent It (7 days later)

**Variation 1:**
> Hey {{name}}, no rush on that testimonial mate. I know you're busy. Whenever you get a chance -- even a quick voice note would be class.

**Variation 2:**
> {{name}}, just a gentle nudge on the testimonial -- no pressure at all. If it's easier, I can write something up based on our conversations and you just tell me if it's accurate?

---

### Video Testimonial Ask

**Variation 1:**
> {{name}}, this might be a big ask, but would you be up for recording a quick video testimonial?
>
> Nothing produced. Just your phone, 30-60 seconds, talking about your experience. You could answer those same 3 questions:
> - Where you were before
> - What's changed
> - What you'd say to someone thinking about it
>
> Video is by far the most powerful form of social proof. Totally understand if that's not your thing though -- the written one is still brilliant.

**Variation 2:**
> Hey {{name}}, random question -- how would you feel about doing a quick video chat with me where I ask you a couple of questions about your experience? Like a mini interview.
>
> I'd record it (with your permission obviously) and use clips on my page. Takes about 5 minutes and you don't need to prepare anything.

**Variation 3:**
> {{name}}, I'm putting together some short testimonial videos. Would you be game? I'll jump on a Zoom with you, ask 3-4 questions, and edit it down to a 60 second clip. You don't have to think about it or prep anything -- I'll lead the whole conversation.

---

## Quick Reference: Response Time Guidelines

| Channel | Ideal Response Time | Maximum Response Time |
|---------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Instagram DM | Under 30 minutes | 2 hours |
| Facebook Messenger | Under 30 minutes | 2 hours |
| WhatsApp | Under 15 minutes | 1 hour |
| Story/Reel replies | Under 1 hour | 4 hours |
| Post-call follow-ups | Within 1 hour | Same day |

**Pro tip:** Set specific times to check DMs (e.g., 8am, 12pm, 5pm, 9pm). Batch your responses rather than checking every 10 minutes.

## Conversation Flow Cheat Sheet

```
Follow / Engagement
        |
   Acknowledge + Ask a Question
        |
   They Reply (learn about them)
        |
   Give Value (tip, guide, or specific advice)
        |
   Qualify (how many players, what stage, what goal)
        |
   Recommend (free guide, strategy call, or specific option)
        |
   Follow Up (if no response after 48 hours)
```

**The rule:** Every message you send should end with a question or a clear next step. Never leave the conversation dead.
