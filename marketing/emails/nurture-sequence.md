Same number of players month after month. Burning out. Wondering what they're doing wrong.

**Here's the #1 mistake: they're coaching, not building.**

The coaches who stay stuck are spending 100% of their energy on what happens ON the pitch and 0% on what happens OFF it.

They've got:
- No system for getting new enquiries every week
- No trial process that converts parents
- No communication rhythm with existing families
- No pricing strategy (usually just copying whoever's nearby)
- No retention system (kids just drift away and they don't know why)

**They're running a coaching session, not a coaching business.**

Sound familiar? It was me for the first year and a half.

**Here's your quick win for today.**

Do this one thing this week. It takes 20 minutes and I guarantee it'll make a difference:

**Send a personal message to every parent on your books.**

Not a group message. A personal one. Use their name. Use their kid's name. Say something specific about their progress.

Something like:

> "Hi Sarah, just wanted to let you know that Jake's been brilliant the last few weeks. His first touch has come on loads and you can see his confidence growing. Really pleased with him. See you Tuesday!"

That's it.

Here's what happens when you do this:
1. That parent feels seen. Their kid feels seen.
2. They screenshot it and send it to their partner/family.
3. They mention it to another parent at school pickup.
4. They don't leave. Because they feel like their kid matters to you (which they should).

**Retention is the fastest way to grow.** Every player you keep is one you don't have to replace.

I know it sounds simple. That's because it is. The problem is nobody does it consistently.

Start there. Build from there.

## Setup Guide: Email Platform Configuration

### Mailchimp Setup

1. **Create an Audience** for lead magnet subscribers
2. **Build a Landing Page** with the opt-in form
3. **Create an Automation** (Customer Journey):
   - Trigger: Joins audience via lead magnet form
   - Add each email as a step with the specified delays
   - Use "Send to" conditions to target non-openers for the re-engagement email
4. **A/B Testing:** Mailchimp supports subject line A/B tests natively. Set 50/50 split with "open rate" as the winning metric.
5. **Segment** non-openers of Emails 5-7 for the re-engagement email

### ConvertKit (Kit) Setup

1. **Create a Form** for the lead magnet
2. **Build a Sequence** with 8 emails (7 + re-engagement)
3. Set delays: 0, 1, 1, 1, 2, 1, 1, 2 days between emails
4. **Use Rules** to tag non-openers and trigger the re-engagement email separately
5. ConvertKit handles A/B subject lines within sequences natively
6. **Create an Automation** to move completed subscribers to your regular newsletter

### MailerLite Setup

1. **Create a Group** for lead magnet subscribers
2. **Build an Automation Workflow**:
   - Trigger: Subscriber joins group
   - Add email steps with delay steps between them
   - Use condition steps (opened/not opened) to branch for re-engagement
3. Use the built-in A/B testing for subject lines (supports up to 3 variations)
4. After the sequence completes, use an action step to move subscribers to your general list

### General Tips (All Platforms)

- **Personalisation:** Use merge tags for first name. "Hey mate" is fine as a fallback if no first name is captured.
- **From name:** "John from PlayIt Love It" or just "John Leitch" -- keep it personal, not branded.
- **Reply-to:** Set to John's actual email. These emails ask for replies -- make sure they go somewhere monitored.
- **Deliverability:** Send the first email immediately to train inboxes that this is wanted email. Engagement on early emails helps later ones land in the inbox.
- **Mobile:** Over 70% will read on mobile. Keep paragraphs short. No wide images. Test on phone before sending.
- **Tracking:** Tag subscribers who click the booking link so you can follow up separately if they don't complete the booking.
- **Post-sequence:** Move completed subscribers to a weekly newsletter list. Don't let them go cold.
